Berlin museum bag rules

Should the bag go into the museum, the cloakroom or a station locker?

Choose the bag, the museum type and your next deadline. The planner gives one practical Berlin move before you lose time at the entrance.

My default rule If it is bigger than a small day bag, solve it before the exhibition.

Bag gate decision

Check first
1 Carry it in

Works only for a genuinely small bag and a relaxed visit.

2 Use cloakroom or locker

The normal move for backpacks, coats, umbrellas and A4-plus bags.

3 Store it before the museum

Best for suitcases, big backpacks, groups or anything you cannot risk being refused.

Recommended move

Use the museum cloakroom before the exhibition.

A normal day backpack is often too large for exhibition rooms. Arrive a few minutes early, use the cloakroom or lockers, and keep only phone, wallet and ticket with you.

Luggage storage guide Berlin museums map

Museum Island

SMB rules use a 30 x 20 x 10 cm threshold for bags and warn that large storage is limited.

Humboldt Forum

Bags larger than A4 go to the cloakroom or lockers. Larger-than-carry-on luggage may not be accepted.

Topography of Terror

Carry-on luggage lockers are available in the foyer, useful when you visit before a train.

Security museums

Some sites, including the Jewish Museum Berlin, use entrance screening and require large bags at cloakroom.
