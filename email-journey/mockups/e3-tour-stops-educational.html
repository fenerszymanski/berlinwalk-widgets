A day after you booked, a few of the stops we’ll visit, with a hook for each one. Not the full story (you’ll get that on the walk), just enough to set the scene.

Stop 01

The TV Tower (Fernsehturm)

365 metres tall, finished in 1969, built by East Germany as a symbol of socialist progress. On sunny days a cross-shaped reflection appears on the dome. West Berliners called it “the Pope’s revenge,” because the regime that built it had spent decades pulling crosses off churches.

Stop 02

Marx-Engels Forum

Two large bronze figures, Karl Marx seated, Friedrich Engels standing, in a public square in the heart of reunified Berlin. There were serious debates about whether to remove them after 1990. They’re still there. We’ll talk about why.

Stop 03

Berliner Dom

Berlin’s Protestant cathedral, built between 1894 and 1905 by Kaiser Wilhelm II. He wanted a Protestant answer to Rome. It was nearly destroyed in the Second World War, then sat as a ruin for decades while East Germany debated what to do with it. The ceiling inside has 7 million mosaic tiles.

Stop 04

Museum Island

Five museums on a single island in the Spree, built across more than a century, holding the bust of Nefertiti, the Pergamon Altar, and the Ishtar Gate. UNESCO World Heritage since 1999. We won’t go inside, but we’ll walk the edges and I’ll tell you what’s worth coming back for.

In total we’ll see 12 stops over about 2 hours, ending at Hackescher Markt. The pace is comfortable, the route mostly flat.
