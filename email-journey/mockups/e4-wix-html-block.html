Three days until we walk Berlin together. Here’s the practical side.

01

Shoes

The most important thing. We cover about 3 kilometres, mostly on flat paved ground, but Berlin has stretches of cobblestones (especially around Museum Island and Hackescher Markt) that punish thin soles. Trainers or any comfortable walking shoe work well. Heels do not.

02

Clothes

The tour runs rain or shine. Check the forecast the day before and layer accordingly. Berlin weather can shift in an afternoon, so a light waterproof jacket is rarely a bad idea, even in summer. In winter: a warm coat, hat, and gloves. We pause often, which means you stand still, which means you get cold faster than you’d expect.

03

Bring

• A water bottle. There are public drinking fountains along the route.

• Your phone or a camera (Museum Island in particular is photogenic).

• A small amount of cash for the end-of-tour tip if you’d like to (cards work too via my QR code, but cash is simpler).

If the weather turns

The tour only cancels in severe weather (storm warning, extreme cold). If that happens, I’ll email you the day before.
