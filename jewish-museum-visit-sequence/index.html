One connected path

Follow the building before the checklist

The minute windows update with the rail above. Treat them as turnaround points, not a demand to rush sensitive spaces.

  1. 01
    Essential buffer 20 minutes

    Arrive with buffer, not speed

    Have your booking confirmation ready, clear security, and use the cloakroom before trying to orient yourself. Starting calm is more useful than winning five minutes here.

    Mandatory: security screening applies to every visitor. Large bags must go to the cloakroom.
    Start +0m, move on by +20m
  2. 02
    Spatial core 50 minutes

    Read the three axes before chasing exhibits

    Follow the underground axes as one connected argument. Take the Garden of Exile and Holocaust Tower without turning them into boxes to tick, then use the Axis of Continuity to move toward the core exhibition.

    Orientation: the Axis of Exile, Axis of the Holocaust and Axis of Continuity form the underground route. The Axis of Continuity has 82 steps, with lifts available. The Garden of Exile is not wheelchair accessible.
    From +20m to +1h 10m
  3. 03
    Longest block 80 minutes

    Give the core exhibition the longest block

    Move through a coherent chronological stretch instead of sampling every display. The core exhibition spans 1,700 years of Jewish life in Germany. It is not only an exhibition about the Holocaust.

    Useful frame: protect the breadth of the story. If time tightens, reduce display depth rather than skipping the historical arc.
    From +1h 10m to +2h 30m
  4. 04
    Protected pause 15 minutes

    Make room to absorb it

    Use the Glass Courtyard or another calm public area to stop, then look back at how Libeskind's Between the Lines design has shaped the route. This is reflection time, not leftover time.

    Accessibility: public areas are otherwise accessible, with the Garden of Exile as the important exception.
    From +2h 30m to +2h 45m
Before you enter

Keep the practical details simple

  • 01Book a timed slot when possible. Walk-ins are accepted, but a slot removes one uncertainty from the day.
  • 02The core exhibition is free. Temporary exhibitions or special programmes may have separate conditions.
  • 03Pack for security. Keep large bags out of the visit path and expect the cloakroom before the exhibition.

Check the official visitor page on the day

Opening conditions, timed slots and temporary closures can change. The museum's own site is the final source. For broader planning, pair this sequence with BerlinWalk's free-museum and museum-bag guides.
