BerlinWalk mini walk

Where to Notice Berlin's Wide Streets

Use these five stops as a street-level decoder: old Berlin, wartime gaps, GDR planning and the monumental scale of Karl-Marx-Allee all line up in one walk.

1

Alexanderplatz

Start with the feeling of scale: wide crossings, long sightlines, tram space and buildings set far apart.

Scale
2

St. Mary's Church and Rathausforum

The church once sat inside a dense quarter. Today it stands almost alone in the open space created after the old street plan vanished.

Old core
3

Karl-Liebknecht-Strasse

This axis shows how old routes were widened, simplified and pulled into a new central plan after the war.

Axis
4

Strausberger Platz

The city suddenly becomes ceremonial. This was not just traffic planning; it was a stage for a new socialist capital.

Power
5

Frankfurter Tor

End between the twin towers and look back west. The boulevard's width finally makes sense as architecture, politics and theatre.

Boulevard
Best done from Alexanderplatz toward Frankfurter Tor. The full walk is about 2.5 km, with U5 stations along the way if you want to shorten it.
