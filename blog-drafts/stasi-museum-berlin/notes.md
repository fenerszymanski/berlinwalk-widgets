## Stasi Museum (Research and Memorial Center Normannenstraße), House 1
- Address: Ruschestraße 103, 10365 Berlin-Lichtenberg (former MfS HQ).
- Hours: Mon–Fri 10:00–18:00; Sat/Sun/holidays 11:00–18:00.
- Admission: €12 adult; €9 reduced; €6 age 13+; €5 school groups.
- English guided tour: 3 pm on weekdays (Mon–Sat), €5 + admission.
- Transport: U5 Magdalenenstraße (~300 m), ~15 min from Alexanderplatz.
- Highlight: Erich Mielke's preserved office suite; surveillance devices,
  disguises, hidden cameras; the ministry's own rooms.
- Self-guided (audio/text); tours optional.
- Sources: stasimuseum.de/en/enbesuch.htm ; berlin.de museum page.

## Berlin-Hohenschönhausen Memorial (the Stasi remand prison)
- Address: Genslerstraße 66, Berlin-Lichtenberg. Tram M5 (also M6) to Genslerstraße.
- Open daily 10:00–18:00.
- Interior is guided-tour ONLY (you cannot wander the cell blocks alone).
- Tours led by former political prisoners / historians; ~1.5 h + intro film.
- English tours several times daily (reported 10:40, 12:40, 14:40 — verify on
  the official ticket page as times shift seasonally).
- Price: €9 standard, €5 concession, €2.50 students. Online booking recommended.
- Source: stiftung-hsh.de/en/ (visit, guided-tours, tickets).

## Stasi "by the numbers" (widget data — citable)
- Founded 8 Feb 1950; dissolved 13 Jan 1990.
- 1989: 91,015 full-time employees.
- Unofficial informants (inoffizielle Mitarbeiter, IM): ~173,081 inside the GDR
  in 1989 (+1,553 in West Germany); a later Müller-Enbergs study puts 1989 IMs
  at ~189,000. Use "about 189,000" with the caveat that estimates vary.
- Ratio: ~1 full-time officer per 166 citizens (vs Gestapo ~1 per 2,000).
  The often-quoted "1 per 6.5 people counting part-time informers" is an
  upper-bound estimate and is contested by historians — present it as such,
  do not state it as plain fact.
- Files: kept on ~6 million people (more than a third of the population).
- Shredded records: ~16,000 sacks of hand-torn documents (~45 million pages)
  survive; only a few hundred sacks reassembled by hand after many years.
- Sources: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stasi ; Britannica; BStU/Stasi Records Archive.
