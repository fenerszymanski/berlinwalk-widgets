## Rejected, and why

- **`05-kolonnenweg-b-NOTBERLIN.jpg` and `05-kolonnenweg-c-NOTBERLIN.jpg`.**
  Both are textbook shots of the perforated concrete slab Kolonnenweg, and both
  are from the inner-German border in Thuringia and Hesse, not Berlin. Using
  either to illustrate the Berlin ring would be geographically false, so both
  are rejected despite being the best surface images available. There is
  currently no usable free photo of the slab Kolonnenweg on the Berlin
  Mauerweg. The whole `Kolonnenweg` category plus every
  `Berliner Mauerweg in <district>` subcategory was checked. **Open item: the
  cleanest fix is Yusuf shooting the Nordgraben or Rudow slab stretch himself.**
- **`04-mauerweg-sign-a.jpg`.** The only waymarker shot with genuinely legible
  lettering, but it is GFDL 1.2 only, not Creative Commons, which is awkward
  for web reuse. Rejected on license, not on quality. `04-mauerweg-sign-b.jpg`
  was used instead.
- **`01-bernauer-strasse-b.jpg`.** Strong 10957px overview panorama, but at
  card crop it reads as a road junction rather than a memorial.
- **`01-bernauer-strasse-c.jpg`.** Beautiful sunrise shot of the steel posts,
  but it shows no concrete, so it undersells the one section where the Wall
  genuinely survives.
- **`02-schlesischer-busch-b.jpg`.** Graffitied wall fragment beside the tower.
  Relevant but cluttered and lower resolution than the chosen frame.
- **`03-east-side-gallery-b.jpg` and `-c.jpg`.** Both are close on single
  murals; the article needs the length of the wall, not one artwork.
- **`06-nieder-neuendorf-b.jpg`.** Near duplicate of the chosen frame with a
  flatter angle.
- **`07-glienicker-bruecke-a.jpg` and `-b.jpg`.** Both good, both unused. The
  Griebnitzsee section of the article ends up recommending readers treat that
  stretch as a Potsdam day trip rather than a Wall walk, so leading it with a
  handsome bridge photo would pull against the advice.
