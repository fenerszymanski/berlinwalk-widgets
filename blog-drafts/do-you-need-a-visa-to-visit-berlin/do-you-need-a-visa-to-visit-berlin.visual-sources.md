## Rationale
- Cover: the Brandenburg Gate reads instantly as "Berlin" at card size and sets a welcoming tone for an entry/paperwork topic without looking bureaucratic. Clean, empty morning frame, professionally shot.
- EES kiosks: a rare, genuinely on-topic image of the actual Entry/Exit System kiosks. This is the freshest, most valuable illustration in the post and directly supports the 2026 border-change section. Photographed at a Spanish airport (Valencia); captioned generically ("kiosks like these") so it is illustrative, not misrepresented as BER.
- BER arrivals: real Berlin arrival context for the practical "arrive early, allow a buffer" advice; ties the abstract rules back to the reader's first hour in the city.
- EU passport: clean, well-lit biometric passport (chip symbol visible) to anchor the passport-validity and visa-free discussion. Generic EU document used purely as an illustration of a biometric travel document.
- Berlin dusk skyline: strong "you made it, now enjoy Berlin" closing image and a natural bridge to the walking-tour connection.

## Rejected
- Schengen Area map (several available): risk of showing an outdated membership map (Romania/Bulgaria joined Schengen in 2025); avoided to prevent a subtly wrong factual visual.
- Automated border e-gate, Munich airport (File:Man using the automatic gate in Munich airport 02.jpg, CC BY-SA 4.0): on-topic but a dull, cluttered older phone photo and redundant with the stronger EES kiosks shot. Downloaded then replaced.
- German "passport" search results were Nazi-era Ahnenpass documents — inappropriate; not used.
- Generic foreign passport-control counter photos (various airports): weaker and less clearly relevant than the EES kiosks image.
