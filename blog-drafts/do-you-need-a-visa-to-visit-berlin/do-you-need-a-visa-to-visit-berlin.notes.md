## Verified facts (sources checked 2026-07-18)
- Berlin is in Germany; Germany is in the Schengen Area. No separate "Berlin visa."
- Visa-exempt nationals (US, UK, CA, AU, NZ, JP, KR, Singapore, Israel, most of Latin America, ~60 total) may enter visa-free for tourism, max 90 days in any rolling 180-day period.
- 90/180: cumulative across ALL Schengen countries; 180-day window is rolling (look back 180 days on any given day); short exits do not reset it. Overstay -> fines / entry ban.
- Non-visa-exempt nationals: need a Schengen short-stay (type C) visa; apply at the German mission for their region (appointment + docs). Apply early.
- EES (Entry/Exit System): FULLY OPERATIONAL across Schengen since 10 April 2026. BER began EES registration from ~2 Dec 2025. First entry: self-service kiosk/booth records facial image + fingerprints + passport data instead of a stamp; later trips faster. Nothing to apply/pay for; automatic at the border. Real effect = longer passport queues in 2026, esp. peak summer -> arrive-early / buffer advice.
- ETIAS: NOT yet in force as of mid-2026. Expected Q4 2026 launch with grace period before mandatory. Travel authorization (not a visa) for 59 visa-exempt nationalities; apply online before travel; approvals usually within hours (up to 96h). Fee 20 EUR. Valid 3 years or until passport expires. Under-18 and over-70 fee-exempt but must still apply. Warn against "apply now" scam sites — system not open yet.
- Passport rule of thumb: valid >= 3 months beyond departure, issued within last 10 years.
