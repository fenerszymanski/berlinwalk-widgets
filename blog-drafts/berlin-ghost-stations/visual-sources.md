## Rejected, with reasons

- `Brandenburger_Tor_U-bahn_-_empty_track.JPG` (Pudelek, CC BY-SA 3.0) —
  **REJECTED AS MISLEADING.** Visually the strongest candidate (an empty
  platform reading as a "ghost station"), but the file description and EXIF
  confirm it is the Brandenburger Tor **U-Bahn** station photographed in
  December 2011. That station opened on the U55 in 2009. The Brandenburger Tor
  ghost station was the **S-Bahn** platform in the north-south tunnel. Using it
  would misidentify the station to the reader.
- `Berlin-Bornholmerstraße_1982.jpg` (Gerd Danigel, CC BY-SA 4.0) — good
  content, but the horizon is tilted roughly 10 degrees and it reads as an
  accidental snapshot. Superseded by the level 1984 frame from the same
  photographer.
- `Blick_auf_Boesebruecke_30.JPG` (Angela M. Arnold, CC BY-SA 3.0) — the bridge
  is obscured by trees and a tangle of overhead wires under flat grey light.
  Does not read at card size.
- `Map_of_Berlin_ghost_stations_(U6_U8_S1_S25)_en.svg` (Ericmetro, CC0) —
  accurate and free, but redundant. The post's own interactive widget is a
  richer version of the same schematic, and a static duplicate would weaken it.
- Bundesarchiv Bild 183 series (Bornholmer Straße Nov 1989, Friedrichstraße
  Apr 1990) — the most iconic historical frames, but the Commons donations are
  capped around 780-800px wide, below the usable master size for this layout.
- `Nordbahnhof Sign.jpg`, `U-Bahn station Alexanderplatz, Berlin.jpg` — tight
  crops of station name signage only, no scene.
