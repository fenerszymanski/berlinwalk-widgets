## Source facts checked on 2026-07-06

Primary / official:
- Berlin.de Low-Emission Zone: https://www.berlin.de/en/tourism/travel-information/1760452-2862820-environmental-zone.en.html
  - Berlin's low-emission zone is in central Berlin and limited by the S-Bahn Ring.
  - Since January 2010, only vehicles with a green sticker have been allowed in the zone.
  - Stickers are available from registration authorities, emissions test centers, and Kfz-Innung workshops.
  - Berlin.de lists online order/shipment at 6 euros, direct vehicle registration authority price at 5 euros, private suppliers often 5 to 15 euros.
  - Sticker validity is not time-limited unless registration changes.
  - Electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids with an E license plate are exempt; foreign electric vehicles may need a blue E sticker if not otherwise marked.
- LABO online application page: https://www.berlin.de/labo/mobilitaet/kfz-zulassung/feinstaubplakette/shop.86595.en.php
  - Foreign vehicles can apply online.
  - Delivery outside Germany can take about 7 to 14 days.
  - The current online page showed 5.95 EUR including VAT when checked.
  - Foreign vehicles need registration papers/technical documents uploaded.
- VIZ Berlin low-emission zone: https://viz.berlin.de/en/umwelt/low-emission-zone/
  - To enter the area within the S-Bahn Ring, vehicles need the green sticker.
  - Vehicle eligibility can be checked via the key number in the vehicle documents.
  - Stickers are issued by LABO, testing authorities, and authorized workshops.
- VIZ Berlin parking/P+R map: https://viz.berlin.de/en/traffic-in-berlin/parking/
  - VIZ provides a map for Park and Ride facilities, public car parks, and private car parks in Berlin.
- German Federal Environment Ministry: https://www.bundesumweltministerium.de/en/topics/air/emissions-control-sticker-and-low-emission-zone
  - Stickers are valid for all low emission zones in Germany.
  - Stickers are mandatory only for vehicles driving into those zones; they are not required just to cross the German border.
