# Visual sources — kurfurstendamm-berlin

All 7 article images are real photographs from Wikimedia Commons under CC BY or
CC BY-SA. Reader-facing attribution is required, so a public **Image credits**
block is included at the end of the post body. No AI visuals were used for
article images or for the widget; the AI allowance for this post is reserved for
the BerlinTools icon only.

Masters optimized to 1600px long edge, JPEG quality 86, progressive.
`images/raw/` is gitignored and holds the untouched originals (rebuildable from
the direct URLs in `images/sources.json`). `images/cand/` was the temporary
candidate pool for the contact sheet and is also gitignored; it was deleted
after selection.

## Contact-sheet judgment

20 candidates were downloaded at 900px and viewed as one local contact sheet
(`images/contact-sheet.jpg`) before selection. The final set was reviewed again
as `images/final-contact-sheet.jpg`, and the widget crops as
`images/widget-stops-sheet.jpg`.

**Cover — Breitscheidplatz in summer.** Chosen because it is the only candidate
that reads instantly at listing-card crop: blue sky, green trees, the broken
tower unmistakable, and the boulevard visible underneath it. The alternative
close-ups of the tower alone were stronger as architecture but did not show that
this is a street, which is what the article is about.

**Rejected, with reasons:**

- Breitscheidplatz Christmas-market night shots (several, all good quality):
  rejected as event-specific. They would misrepresent a year-round guide.
- Two winter Kurfürstendamm street views with bare trees and grey dusk: legal
  and sharp but visually flat, and they make the street look worse than it is.
- A dated aerial over Europa-Center: cluttered, hard to read, and the skyline has
  changed since.
- Two Savignyplatz frames that were mostly empty lawn: pretty but they said
  nothing about why the square is worth the detour. The chosen frame shows the
  square and the surrounding apartment buildings together.
- A first Bikini Berlin pick (church tower plus signage, large empty sky) was
  downloaded, optimized, then swapped out during final review for the blue-hour
  facade shot, which reads as an actual building rather than a signpost.
- A 1958 Kurfürstendamm street scene: strong archive image but vertical and
  less relevant than the 1978 frame, which shows the axis as a working West
  Berlin downtown.
