## Historical source ledger

### Main official source

- **Landesdenkmalamt Berlin, Mehringplatz, object 09097784,T**
  https://denkmaldatenbank.berlin.de/daobj.php?obj_dok_nr=09097784%2CT

Facts used from this record:

- The present Mehringplatz began with the 1730s Friedrichstadt extension and the square then called the Rondell.
- Wilhelmstraße, Friedrichstraße and Lindenstraße met at the square by Hallesches Tor.
- The name Belle-Alliance-Platz dates to 1815.
- The Friedenssäule and its crowning figure date to 1840–1843; the broader square took shape in 1839–1843, with landscape work by Peter Joseph Lenné.
- The customs wall was removed and Johann Heinrich Strack's gatehouses helped form the late-19th-century southern entrance.
- The elevated railway dates to 1902; the U-Bahn work cited by the record dates to 1923–1925.
- Wartime destruction was followed by complete clearance visible in postwar aerial photographs and an open site throughout the 1950s.
- Hans Scharoun won the 1962 competition, retained but reduced the Rondell and diverted car traffic around a pedestrian centre.
- Werner Düttmann took over the larger second phase in 1968, retaining concentric rings while adapting the programme to housing and shops.

### Editorial limits

- The photographs do not share a verified camera position or lens.
- The viewer therefore uses labelled side-by-side evidence, never an opacity overlay or a claim of pixel-perfect alignment.
- The package contains no generated historical reconstruction.
- Exact creator and licence details must be rechecked on the source pages immediately before publication.
