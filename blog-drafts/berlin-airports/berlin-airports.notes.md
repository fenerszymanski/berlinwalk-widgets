## Verified facts (sources rechecked 2026-07-18)
- Berlin's scheduled passenger traffic is concentrated at Berlin Brandenburg Airport "Willy Brandt" (code BER). T1 opened on 31 October 2020; Tegel handled its final flight on 8 November 2020.
- Terminals in use: Terminal 1 (main; current list includes Lufthansa and easyJet) and Terminal 2 (current list includes Ryanair and Wizz Air). Airline assignments can change, so the booking/live flight information is authoritative.
- T1 and T2 are about a five-minute signed walk apart. The rail station is directly under T1 on level U2; T2 passengers follow the level E0 passage.
- Terminal 5 was the former Schönefeld Airport terminal. It closed in 2021, and FBB decided in November 2022 that it would not reopen.
- Tegel (TXL): final flight 8 November 2020. The hexagonal terminal remains; the site is being redeveloped as the Urban Tech Republic and Schumacher Quartier.
- Schönefeld remains a place and an S-Bahn station, but SXF is not a separate passenger airport.
- Tempelhof Airport closed in 2008. Tempelhofer Feld opened as a free public park in 2010; barbecues are limited to designated areas.
- Current airport rail service after the 14 December 2025 timetable change:
  - FEX: Berlin Hbf via Potsdamer Platz and Südkreuz to BER, ~23 minutes Hbf–BER, about every 15 minutes through most of the day.
  - Regional lines: RE20, RB22, RB24 and RB32. RE20 uses the central FEX corridor; RB24/RB32 connect via Ostkreuz.
  - S-Bahn: S9 and S85 every ~20 minutes. S9 reaches Alexanderplatz/Stadtbahn; continuous overnight S9 service applies on Friday and Saturday nights, not every night.
  - Taxi: usually ~35–45 minutes; practical central-Berlin estimate €55–65 (Berlin.de examples: about €58 to Alexanderplatz and €63 to Zoo). Ride-hailing apps use live quotes and may differ.
- Ticket: BER is in fare zone C. A 2026 Berlin ABC single costs €5.00 and is valid for 120 minutes in one direction. Validate a paper ticket or activate a digital ticket before travel.
