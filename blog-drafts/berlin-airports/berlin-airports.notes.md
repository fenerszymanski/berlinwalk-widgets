## Verified facts (sources checked 2026-07-17)
- Berlin has ONE commercial airport: Berlin Brandenburg Airport "Willy Brandt" (code BER), ~18 km SE of the centre, fully opened Oct 2020.
- Terminals in use: Terminal 1 (main, most airlines incl. Lufthansa/Star Alliance and many low-cost) and Terminal 2 (adjacent, used at busy times, easyJet and other low-cost). T1 and T2 are a short covered walk apart.
- Terminal 5 = the former Schönefeld Airport (SXF). Permanently closed (since 2025); no flights. Possible future repurposing (events/conference), not a passenger terminal.
- Tegel (TXL): closed permanently November 2020. Iconic hexagon terminal. Site being redeveloped (Urban Tech Republic / Schumacher Quartier).
- Schönefeld (SXF): absorbed into BER as T5, now closed.
- Tempelhof (THF): closed 2008. Now Tempelhofer Feld, a huge free public park on the old runways/apron.
- Old codes TXL and SXF are retired. If a booking site/old blog still shows TXL/SXF, it is outdated — every Berlin flight now lands at BER.
- Getting to the city (rail leaves from station "Flughafen BER – Terminal 1-2", directly under T1/T2):
  - FEX (Flughafen-Express / Airport Express): every ~15 min, ~23 min to Berlin Hauptbahnhof (via Ostkreuz, Gesundbrunnen). ~04:00–01:00.
  - Regional trains RE7, RE8, RB14, RB22, RB23 also serve BER toward central/other stations.
  - S-Bahn S9: to the eastern city and on to Spandau, every ~20 min, day and night; slower (~45–50 min to Hauptbahnhof) but useful for eastern destinations. (S45 no longer operates.)
  - Taxi / ride-hail: ~30–45 min to the centre, roughly €50–60 depending on traffic/destination.
- Ticket: BER is in fare zone C, so any rail trip to/from the airport needs a Berlin ABC ticket. 2026 ABC single = €5.00 (regular). Buy from a BVG/VBB machine or app before boarding; validate paper tickets.
