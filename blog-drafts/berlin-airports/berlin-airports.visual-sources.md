## Rationale
- Cover: a clean, symmetrical view of BER T1 and its entrance plaza. The daylight architecture stays legible after the 16:9 cover crop.
- T2: shows the road and buildings between the adjacent terminal areas, supporting the "one airport, two terminals" section without implying that T2 is a separate airport.
- FEX: the practical "how to reach the city" image, the actual Airport Express train.
- Tempelhofer Feld: the strongest single image of "a closed Berlin airport you can still visit" — runway now a park.
- Tegel: a clear exterior view of the former terminal, supporting the Tegel section without claiming that the photo shows the full hexagonal layout.

## Rejected
- Various interior/gate shots of T1: utilitarian, weak as editorial images.
- Tempelhof portrait full-frame: cropped to landscape for body/gallery use.
- Aerial BER shots: less legible than the facade for a card/cover.
- Earlier T1 and T2 candidates by Arne Müseler: rejected because their source-page reuse notice requires always-visible online attribution, which conflicts with the project's compact, default-closed Image credits disclosure.
