## Public caption plan

1. `The public entrance is in the baroque Old Building. The route then continues underground into Libeskind's zinc-clad building.`
2. `Every visit starts here with mandatory security, even though the core exhibition is in the Libeskind Building.`
3. `The Garden of Exile tilts the ground and your sense of balance. It is part of the architecture, not a decorative courtyard.`
4. `The Holocaust Tower is cold, quiet and lit by one high slit. Stay only as long as feels right.`
5. `The Glass Courtyard is a useful place to pause before or after the longer core exhibition.`
6. `The core exhibition has occupied the Libeskind Building since its 2020 redesign, and its story reaches far beyond the architecture.`

## Rejected candidates

- `Jewish Museum Berlin 02.JPG` / Shalekhet: strong image, but the underlying installation may still carry artwork copyright. Rejected to avoid an unnecessary rights ambiguity.
- `Libeskind-Bau (Jüdisches Museum Berlin) Nutzung.jpg` by Akiramifune, CC BY 4.0: rejected from the article and widget because its vertical crop created a weak desktop card and the 2026 core-exhibition photograph is more current and informative.
- Generic zinc-facade close-ups: legal and sharp but too repetitive and visually cold for a six-image article set.
- Older exhibition photographs from before the 2020 core-exhibition redesign: rejected because they could misrepresent what visitors see in 2026.
- Images dominated by event crowds, temporary signage, construction, or protest context: rejected as date-specific or misleading for an evergreen visitor guide.
- Exterior photos that showed only the Libeskind facade: rejected for the cover because visitors actually enter through the Old Building, and the paired-building view is clearer.
