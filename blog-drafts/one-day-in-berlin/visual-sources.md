# Visual sources — One Day in Berlin

All images are genuine, license-verified Wikimedia Commons photographs, reused
from earlier BerlinWalk posts where the license and provenance were already
checked. No AI-generated or code-drawn visuals were used in this post. Optimized
files (long edge ~1600px, JPEG) live in `images/optimized/`.

The image set follows the arc of a single day on the central historic spine:
the Museum Island / TV Tower cover, a quiet-morning Brandenburg Gate, the free
Reichstag dome, an afternoon at the East Side Gallery, and a slow Spree sunset.
Every caption says what the image really is.

## Cover choice
`cover-museum-island-tv-tower.jpg` is the featured/cover image: Museum Island
with the TV Tower rising behind it, across the Spree. It reads instantly as
"central Berlin, one walkable area" at listing-card crop, which is exactly the
promise of a one-day itinerary that keeps you on the historic spine.

## Reader-facing Image Credits
The cover, Brandenburg Gate, Reichstag and Spree images use CC BY-SA licenses
that require attribution, so a reader-facing **Image credits** block is included
at the foot of the published body. The East Side Gallery image is CC0 (no
attribution required) but is credited for completeness.

## Rejected
- Generic wide skyline stock with no route/area context — reads as "a city",
  not "the walkable centre you spend your day in".
- Crowded selfie-heavy Brandenburg Gate frames — the quiet-morning frame matches
  the article's "go early" advice and is more legible at card size.
- A separate Berliner Dom / Museum Island frame was copied in but dropped to keep
  five well-distributed images instead of clustering two Museum Island shots.
