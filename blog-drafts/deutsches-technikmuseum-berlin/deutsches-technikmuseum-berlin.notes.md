## Verified facts (official technikmuseum.berlin, checked 2026-07-23)

- Hours: Monday closed; Tue–Fri 9:00–17:30; Sat–Sun and most public holidays
  10:00–18:00; last admission 17:00. Open 313 days/year. Closed 1 May, 24/25
  Dec, 31 Dec; 1 Jan opens 13:00–18:00.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/visit/opening-hours/
- Prices: adult EUR 15 online / EUR 17 at the door (EUR 2 service fee);
  reduced EUR 9 online / EUR 11 door; groups of 5+ eligible people EUR 6 pp
  online; under-18 free (a 0-euro ticket is still required); annual ticket
  EUR 55 / EUR 30 reduced; Museum Pass Berlin accepted. Free for everyone on
  the first Friday of each month from 13:00, online booking required.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/visit/prices/
- One ticket covers both the Technikmuseum and the Science Center Spectrum on
  the same day. Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/visit/faq/
- Average visit ~2.5 hours per official FAQ; lockers in main building and at
  Ladestraße entrance; no cloakroom; photography without tripod/flash OK
  non-commercial; dogs no (except assistance dogs).
- Addresses: main building Trebbiner Straße 9, 10963 Berlin; Ladestraße and
  Science Center Spectrum entrance Möckernstraße 26, 10963 Berlin.
  Transit: U Möckernbrücke (U1/U3/U7), U Gleisdreieck (U1/U2/U3),
  S Anhalter Bahnhof (S1/S2/S25/S26). Parking very limited, fees apply from
  1 Aug 2026; museum recommends transit.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/visit/getting-here/
- Aviation: New Building, "From Ballooning to the Berlin Airlift", 40+
  aircraft. Douglas C-47 Skytrain (Rosinenbomber) mounted over the roof
  terrace in May 1999, symbol of the 1948/49 Berlin Airlift. Jeannin
  Stahltaube (1914) oldest aircraft in collection; Ju 52 "Aunt Ju"; Ju 87
  Stuka wreck shown deliberately as a wreck.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/exhibitions/permanent-exhibition/aviation/
- Rail: two roundhouses (1874) of the former Anhalter Güterbahnhof, ~6,000 m²
  with outdoor turntables; ~40 original rail vehicles; oldest preserved
  streetcar = Berlin horse tram 1865; 1843 open 3rd-class passenger car;
  E 19 streamlined electric loco (1938, 180 km/h); a boxcar of the type used
  in Holocaust deportations.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/exhibitions/permanent-exhibition/rail-transport/
- Shipping: ~6,500 m² over three levels, 1,100+ objects, "one of the world's
  largest" ship exhibitions; steam tug KURT HEINZ (worked Berlin waterways
  until 1997); ~1840 Kaffenkahn barge salvaged from the Havel (largest
  object); one-man Biber submarine (1944).
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/exhibitions/permanent-exhibition/shipping/
- Road transport: "On the Move" exhibition in the Ladestraße building; Benz
  21/50 (1914), VW Beetle, Amphicar (1966), early electric Slaby-Beringer
  (1921), eleven themed sections.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/exhibitions/permanent-exhibition/road-transport/
- Computers: Konrad Zuse's Z1 reconstruction, built by Zuse for the museum in
  1989. Sources: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/exhibitions/permanent-exhibition/computers/
  and museum-digital object 111992.
- Spectrum: three-storey brick building at Möckernstraße 26, eight themed
  experiment zones (light, sound, mechanics, electricity...).
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/spectrum/
- History: opened December 1983 as Museum für Verkehr und Technik on the
  Anhalter Güterbahnhof site; New Building (~12,000 m²) opened ~2001, shipping
  2003, aviation 2005. Museum Park: two windmills, historical brewery, forge.
  Source: https://technikmuseum.berlin/en/about-us/our-history/
- Special exhibitions named 2026: Carbon Culture (until 27 Sep 2026),
  SuperCity 3000 (27 Mar 2026 – 27 Jun 2027). Not central to the article.
