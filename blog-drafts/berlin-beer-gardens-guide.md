## Quick Summary

- Berlin's beer gardens (Biergärten) are a summer and shoulder-season staple, typically open from April/May to September/October.
- **Prater Biergarten** in Prenzlauer Berg is Berlin's oldest, operating under chestnut trees since 1837.
- For scenery, **Café am Neuen See** offers lakeside views and rowboats in Tiergarten park, while **Zenner** and **Zollpackhof** sit right on the Spree river.
- **BRLO Brwhouse** at Park am Gleisdreieck represents Berlin's modern craft beer scene, built from shipping containers.
- Traditional etiquette: you can bring your own food (but not drinks) to traditional self-service tables in many gardens, though buying local snacks (Pretzels, Obatzda, Bratwurst) is common.
- Most beer gardens are self-service; look for separate windows/lines for food (Essen) and drinks (Getränke), and carry cash as some spots do not accept cards for small orders.
