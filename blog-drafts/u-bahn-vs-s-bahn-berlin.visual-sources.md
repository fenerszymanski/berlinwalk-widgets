## Selection rationale
- Cover: the viaduct shot instantly signals "overground S-Bahn" and is genuinely
  beautiful; Hackescher Markt is also where my tour ends (route relevance).
- 02 Wuhletal is the single clearest teaching image on the whole topic: both
  trains, both colours, one platform. Placed in the "how to tell them apart" section.
- 03/04 give a clean yellow-U vs red-S contrast in the two dedicated sections.
- 05/06 tie the whole system to Alexanderplatz, the tour start and the biggest
  U+S interchange, without being protest/event-specific or cluttered.

## Rejected
- Official BVG/VBB network map: copyrighted, not usable.
- Various low-res or heavily-signage-cropped station shots: weak/cluttered.

## Facts verified (2026-07-11)
- U-Bahn: BVG, 9 lines U1–U9, ~175 stations, mostly underground.
- S-Bahn: S-Bahn Berlin (Deutsche Bahn), ~16 lines, ~168 stations, mostly overground,
  reaches Brandenburg/Potsdam; Ringbahn = S41 clockwise, S42 anticlockwise.
- One VBB/AB ticket covers U-Bahn, S-Bahn, tram, bus; ABC for BER/Potsdam.
- Night: both run 24h Fri/Sat; weekdays ~4:00–1:00 with N-buses filling gaps.
Sources: en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin_U-Bahn, /Berlin_S-Bahn, /Berlin_Ringbahn; berlin.de public-transport pages.
