## Cover rationale

Körnerpark is the strongest "hidden Neukölln reveal": a sunken neo-baroque
garden 5-7 m below street level that almost nobody expects behind Neukölln's
loud streets. It is instantly distinctive, reads well at listing-card crop,
and carries the whole thesis of the post (Neukölln is not one thing; the good
parts are the ones you have to know about). Landscape 1.32 ratio.

## Considered and rejected

- koernerpark-b (Körnerpark0048) — cherub-on-crocodile fountain detail: charming
  but a detail shot, not establishing. Rejected (redundant second Körnerpark).
- koernerpark-c (Bild 7) — close-up stone putto in autumn: too tight/seasonal.
- rixdorf-b (Neukölln Richardplatz-001) — the cobbled square: good orientation
  but the smithy (rixdorf-a) is more characterful and memorable. Rejected as
  redundant second Rixdorf image.
- maybachufer-canal (Landwehrkanal) — canal-side terrace: fine, but the market
  stall image carries more energy and the canal is covered by the existing
  Maybachufer-market post. Rejected to avoid a second Maybachufer image.
- rathaus (Rathaus Neukölln tower) — handsome civic landmark, but overlaps
  Hermannplatz as a "civic street" shot and the Karl-Marx-Straße section is
  already carried by the Klunkerkranich rooftop image. Rejected as redundant.
