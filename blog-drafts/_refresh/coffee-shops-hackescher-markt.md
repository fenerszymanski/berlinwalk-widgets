# 5 Best Coffee Shops Near Hackescher Markt

Hackescher Markt is one of the easiest places in Berlin to need a coffee.

You might have just finished a walking tour. You might be coming out of Hackesche Hoefe. You might be between Museum Island, Alexanderplatz and the old Jewish quarter, with tired feet and no patience for a bad cappuccino.

The good news is simple: this part of Mitte has some excellent coffee.

The bad news is also simple: it has plenty of tourist-trap coffee too.

This guide is for the moment when you want to choose quickly and well. Not every place here is the cheapest. Not every place is ideal for laptops. But each one is worth knowing for a different reason.

## Before You Choose: What Kind of Coffee Break Do You Need?

Hackescher Markt is not one single mood.

It can be noisy around the S-Bahn arches, pretty inside Hackesche Hoefe, relaxed on side streets, and surprisingly touristy on the obvious corners. The best cafe depends on what you need in that moment.

Ask yourself one question first: do you want **coffee as the main event**, or do you want **somewhere to sit and recover**?

Those are not always the same place.

For serious coffee, choose The Barn, Ben Rahim or Bonanza. For brunch, choose Father Carpenter. For cake, choose Princess Cheesecake. For a fast post-tour reset, choose the closest place that matches your energy and do not over-optimise.

One practical note: opening hours and menus change. I checked the current location pages while refreshing this guide, but you should still tap through before making a special detour, especially on holidays.

## 1. The Barn Hackescher Markt - Best for Serious Specialty Coffee

[The Barn Hackescher Markt](https://thebarn.de/pages/locations/the-barn-hackescher-markt) is the safest pick if your main goal is coffee quality.

It sits on **Neue Schoenhauser Strasse 12**, a few minutes from Hackescher Markt, in the old Scheunenviertel. The Barn's own location page lists this cafe as its Hackescher Markt branch and gives current opening hours.

The space is small, clean and focused. It does not feel like a cosy living room. It feels like a coffee bar run by people who care very much about beans, water, extraction and whether you should really be putting sugar into that cup.

That can be exactly what you want.

The Barn helped define Berlin's modern specialty coffee scene. The Hackescher Markt location is useful because it gives you that level of coffee right in the tourist centre, without needing to travel to a more residential neighbourhood.

Choose it for:

- **Flat white, espresso or filter coffee** where the bean actually matters.
- **A quick, high-quality stop** before or after shopping in Mitte.
- **Beans to take home** if you want a Berlin souvenir you will actually use.

Skip it if you want a long lazy brunch, big sofas, or a place where nobody cares how long you occupy a table.

My honest read: The Barn is not the warmest cafe on this list. It is the most dependable coffee-first choice.

## 2. Ben Rahim - Best Hidden Courtyard Atmosphere

[Ben Rahim](https://benrahim.com/) is the most memorable coffee stop near Hackescher Markt.

It is tucked inside **Hackesche Hoefe**, the Art Nouveau courtyard complex that every visitor should see at least once. Ben Rahim's own story says the brand began in Berlin with its first coffee shop in Hackesche Hoefe in 2015, and specialty coffee guide Kaffetid lists the address as **Hackesche Hoefe, Sophienstrasse 7A**.

The atmosphere is the opposite of Berlin minimalism.

Inside, you get rugs, patterned tiles, a North African influence and the sense that you have stepped out of Mitte for a moment. It is small, distinctive and easy to remember.

This is also the place on the list where coffee culture feels most specific. Ben Rahim highlights Arabic and Tunisian coffee traditions, including **cezwe**, a finely ground coffee brewed in a small pot. Their own philosophy also says they do not serve sugar and that hand-brewed coffee is served without milk.

That sounds strict, but it makes sense when you understand the place. Ben Rahim is not trying to be a generic cafe. It is trying to show you coffee in a particular way.

Choose it for:

- **A unique Berlin coffee experience**, not just a caffeine stop.
- **Cezwe or hand brew** if you like trying different preparation methods.
- **A beautiful courtyard pause** inside Hackesche Hoefe.

Skip it if you want the cheapest coffee near the station, a giant table, or a very conventional cappuccino-and-cake situation.

My honest read: Ben Rahim is the one I would send someone to if they had already seen plenty of normal European cafes and wanted something with personality.

## 3. Father Carpenter - Best for Brunch and a Real Sit-Down Break

[Father Carpenter](https://fathercarpenter.com/) is the place to choose when coffee alone is not enough.

It is hidden in a courtyard at **Muenzstrasse 21**, close to Hackescher Markt and Alexanderplatz. The official Father Carpenter site lists the Mitte cafe there, and [visitBerlin](https://www.visitberlin.de/en/father-carpenter-coffee-brewers) describes it as Australian coffee culture brought to Berlin, serving specialty coffee, smoothies and pastries in a Muenzstrasse courtyard.

That courtyard matters.

You step off a busy shopping street and suddenly the city gets quieter. It is still central Mitte, but it feels protected from the rush outside.

Father Carpenter is better for sitting down than The Barn or Ben Rahim. It works for a proper brunch, a slower reset, or the moment when your group needs food and coffee at the same time.

Choose it for:

- **Brunch with good coffee** rather than coffee only.
- **A courtyard table** when the weather is good.
- **A calmer pause** after walking through the centre.

Skip it if you only want a fast espresso, if you dislike waiting for tables, or if you need the cheapest possible snack.

My honest read: Father Carpenter is the best recovery stop after a long morning, but it is not the place to rush.

## 4. Bonanza Coffee - Best Calm Coffee Detour

[Bonanza Coffee](https://bonanzacoffee.de/pages/where-to-find) is the practical update to this guide.

A farther-north cafe can still be excellent, but it is less useful when someone is actually standing near Hackescher Markt. Bonanza's **Alte Schoenhauser Strasse 15** location fits this post much better.

Bonanza's own location page lists its Mitte cafe at Alte Schoenhauser Strasse 15. That makes it a better fit for this post.

The mood is serious but less tucked-away than Ben Rahim and less brunch-focused than Father Carpenter. It is a coffee stop for people who want a strong roaster name, a good cup and a short walk away from the most obvious tourist flow.

Choose it for:

- **A quieter specialty coffee detour** in Mitte.
- **Beans or roaster credibility** if you follow Berlin coffee.
- **A good Plan B** when The Barn is too busy.

Skip it if your group mainly wants cake, a big brunch menu, or a hidden courtyard setting.

My honest read: Bonanza is the practical upgrade here. It keeps the guide more useful for people actually standing near Hackescher Markt.

## 5. Princess Cheesecake - Best for Coffee and Cake

[Princess Cheesecake](https://www.princess-cheesecake.de/) is not on this list because it is the most serious coffee destination.

It is here because sometimes you do not need a tasting-note conversation. Sometimes you need a good coffee and a beautiful slice of cheesecake.

The Tucholskystrasse branch is at **Tucholskystrasse 37**, roughly a comfortable walk from Hackescher Markt. The official Princess Cheesecake site lists the address, current opening hours and the fact that the cafe focuses on handmade cakes using carefully selected natural organic products.

The case is the attraction. Classic cheesecake, seasonal versions, chocolate, fruit, smaller pieces, bigger pieces, all arranged in a way that makes self-control feel theatrical.

The coffee is fine. The cake is the reason.

Choose it for:

- **Coffee and cake** after a long walk.
- **A sweet stop** with someone who does not care about specialty coffee.
- **A quieter street** away from the S-Bahn arches.

Skip it if you are looking for the purest espresso, a cheap grab-and-go stop, or a laptop cafe.

My honest read: Princess Cheesecake is the best emotional choice. It is what you pick when the day needs a reward.

## Coffee Culture Tip: Do Not Rush

Berlin cafes are not usually designed for the fastest possible turnover.

Of course, you can grab a takeaway coffee. But if you sit down, especially in a proper cafe, it is normal to linger. You do not have to apologise for finishing slowly. You also do not need to over-tip like you might in North America.

For a cafe counter, a small round-up or coins in the jar are enough if you want to tip. For table service, a modest round-up or around 5 to 10 percent is normal when service was good. I explain the bigger picture in my [Berlin tipping guide](https://www.berlinwalk.com/post/tipping-in-berlin).

Also, payment habits vary. Ben Rahim says it is cashless, while smaller Berlin places can still surprise you the other way. If you are unsure, my [Berlin credit card guide](https://www.berlinwalk.com/post/can-you-use-credit-cards-in-berlin-a-tourist-s-guide-to-paying-in-germany) explains the local payment reality.
