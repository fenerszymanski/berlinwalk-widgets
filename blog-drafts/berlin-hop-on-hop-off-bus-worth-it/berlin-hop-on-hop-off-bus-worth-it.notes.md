## Verified 2026 facts (research day 2026-07-17)
- Hop-on hop-off in Berlin: ~4 operators, ~6 routes. Prices ~€30–€45 for a 24h adult pass; 48h/72h also sold. Children reduced.
  - Big Bus Tours: Red route ~2h loop, Blue route ~40min. Ticket tiers (Discover/Essential/Explore) bundle currywurst, digital/guided walking tours, evening tour, and a Spree cruise (seasonal ~Mar–Nov). Audio in multiple languages.
  - City Sightseeing: Classic Tour ~120 min + "Trendy East / Berlin Wall" Tour ~60 min. Tickets valid ~3 months from booking.
  - Others: City Circle, Tempelhofer Sightseeing.
  - Buses typically run ~09:30–18:00; reduced/seasonal in winter.
- BVG tickets 2026:
  - Single AB (Einzelfahrschein): €4.00 (2h, transfers allowed, not return).
  - Kurzstrecke (short trip): €2.80.
  - 24-Stunden-Karte (day ticket) AB: €11.20; ABC €12.90.
  - Single ABC (incl. airport): €5.00.
  - 7-day ticket discontinued 1 Jan 2026.
- Bus 100 and Bus 200: normal BVG public buses, covered by ANY valid BVG ticket. Route past Reichstag, Brandenburg Gate, Tiergarten/Siegessäule, between Alexanderplatz and Zoologischer Garten. No commentary, but double-decker; top front seats give the sightseeing view for the price of a normal ticket. (See published post: /post/bus-100-berlin-the-4-sightseeing-tour-locals-don-t-want-you-to-know-about)
- Free walking tour: tip-based; a live human guide. BerlinWalk tour starts Alexanderplatz, ~2 hours.

## Honest angle (Yusuf voice)
The hop-on hop-off is not a scam, but for most people in central Berlin it is the expensive way to solve a problem the city already solves cheaply. Central Berlin's big sights sit in a tight line you can walk, and Bus 100/200 covers almost the same corridor for the price of one normal ticket. The bus makes sense for speco cases: limited walking, short time + wide coverage, families who want a fixed base with a roof, or bad weather. Pick by situation, not by habit.
