# Berlin by Foot: Best Walkable Areas for First-Time Visitors

Status: production package in progress.

Focus keyword: `Berlin by foot`
Slug: `berlin-by-foot`
Category: `Tour Route` primary, `Tourist Tips` secondary.
Tool slug: `berlin-walkable-areas-planner`

## Topic Decision

Chosen after API/local inventory because there is no published or unpublished Wix post for this exact search angle. Nearby content exists for Unter den Linden, best views on foot, public transport, where to stay, and a self-guided Alexanderplatz to Hackescher Markt draft, but this post is a broader decision guide: which areas reward walking, and when to use transit between them.

## Search Intent

Tourists planning a first Berlin itinerary want to know whether Berlin is walkable, which areas are best on foot, how much time to allow, and how not to exhaust themselves.

## Widget Ideas Considered

1. `Berlin Walkable Areas Planner` - helps visitors choose one walkable area based on time, energy, weather and priority. This is the chosen tool because it is reusable beyond the article.
2. `Foot vs U-Bahn Decision Strip` - quick yes/no calculator for whether to walk the next connection. Useful but too thin as a BerlinTools entry.
3. `First-Day Step Story Builder` - sentimental route-mood builder around the first 90 minutes in Berlin. Stronger as article copy, less generally useful as a tool.
