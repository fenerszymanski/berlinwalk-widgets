## Source Notes

- Berliner Dom Hohenzollern Crypt: https://www.berlinerdom.de/en/visiting/about-the-cathedral/the-hohenzollern-crypt/
  - Official page says the Hohenzollern Crypt reopened on 1 March 2026 after extensive construction and renovation.
  - It describes the crypt as Germany's most important dynastic burial site and one of Europe's significant dynastic burial sites.
  - It states there are 91 burials from the end of the 16th century to the beginning of the 20th century.
  - Mentions the Great Elector, King Frederick I, Sophie Charlotte and Queen Elisabeth Christine as examples.

- Berliner Dom construction history: https://www.berlinerdom.de/en/visiting/about-the-cathedral/construction-history-up-to-1905/
  - Use later if the Dom section needs more construction detail or Kaiser Wilhelm II context.

- Humboldt Forum main visitor source: https://www.humboldtforum.org/en/
  - Use for the public reader-facing link.
  - Research note: a more direct official English "history of the site" URL should be found during the Wix/package pass. The attempted `history-of-the-site-16574` URL returned 404.

- visitBerlin Brandenburg Gate: https://www.visitberlin.de/en/brandenburg-gate
  - Official tourism page says the gate was constructed between 1788 and 1791, designed by Carl Gotthard Langhans, architect to the Prussian court, and is the only surviving historical city gate.
  - Also frames it as a symbol of division and reunification, useful for the "layered meaning" section.

- visitBerlin Unter den Linden: https://www.visitberlin.de/en/unter-den-linden
  - Existing Unter den Linden draft already sourced this as the official tourism overview for the boulevard from Brandenburg Gate to Schlossbruecke/Humboldt Forum.

- Berlin.de Unter den Linden: https://www.berlin.de/en/attractions-and-sights/3559846-3104052-unter-den-linden.en.html
  - Existing Unter den Linden draft cites it for 1573 bridle path, 1647 linden trees and Frederick the Great boulevard development.

- UNESCO Museum Island: https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/896/
  - The site currently surfaced a JavaScript/cookie page via curl, but this remains the canonical UNESCO World Heritage listing URL to recheck during production.

- SPSG Charlottenburg Palace: https://www.spsg.de/en/palaces-gardens/object/charlottenburg-palace-old-palace/
  - Use during production for official palace visitor context and Sophie Charlotte detail.

- Deutsches Historisches Museum LeMO:
  - Wilhelm II biography: https://www.dhm.de/lemo/biografie/wilhelm-ii
