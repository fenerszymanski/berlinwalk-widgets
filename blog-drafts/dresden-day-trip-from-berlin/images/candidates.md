## Subjects with no usable Commons image found

- **Kronentor and Nymphenbad in one frame** — Commons has each separately but nothing that reads as "the Zwinger courtyard complex" in a single shot.
- **A modern, well-exposed daylight Canaletto view** — this is the real gap. The Commons pool for the classic Neustadt-bank panorama is dominated by 2006/2013 flood documentation, night shots, historic B&W and colourised plates, and heavily processed tree-framed versions. `canaletto-05` is the only usable daylight frame and it still needs a hard crop.
- **Brühlsche Terrasse busy with people in summer** — the terrace shots on Commons are mostly empty, wintry, or snapshot-grade. `bruehlsche-01` is the best available and is still only moderately populated.
- **Fürstenzug with a visible crowd in front of it** — nothing shows the mural with the tour groups that are actually there most of the day.
- **Semperoper interior** — the auditorium is not represented by a usable freely licensed image, so the opera house has to stay an exterior subject.

## Licence caveats to check before publishing

- Every file above is CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC0 or public domain. One GFDL-1.2-only file surfaced during sourcing and was rejected as not practically reusable.
- **The Kunsthofpassage courtyards are the significant rights question.** `kunsthof-01` to `kunsthof-05` photograph facade artworks that are themselves still in copyright (the Hof der Elemente funnel wall, the Hof der Metamorphosen metal relief, the Hof der Tiere animals, and the Hof des Lichts facade, by the artists Annette Paul, Christoph Roßner, André Tempel and others). German freedom of panorama covers works permanently installed on public ways, streets and squares. These are semi-private courtyards reached through a passage, which is exactly the borderline case the exception does not clearly cover. Treat this subject as optional, and if it is used, credit the facade artist alongside the photographer.
- `bruehlsche-04` photographs a temporary "Liebe" installation on the Freitreppe, which is a separate copyright layer and not permanently installed, so freedom of panorama does not apply. Recommend dropping it.
- Always reproduce the exact artist string plus the licence name and a link to the licence when publishing CC BY / CC BY-SA files.
