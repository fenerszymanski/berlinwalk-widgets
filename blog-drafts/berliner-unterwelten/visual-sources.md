# Visual sources — berliner-unterwelten

Post: `Berliner Unterwelten: How to Actually Get Into Berlin's Bunker Tours (2026)`
Slug: `berliner-unterwelten`
Drafted: 2026-07-20 (Europe/Berlin)

42 candidates pulled from Wikimedia Commons, contact sheet built and viewed at
`images/contact-sheet.jpg`, 6 selected. Masters optimized to 1600px long edge at
JPEG quality 86 before Wix upload. `images/raw/` and `images/cand/` are
gitignored; every raw can be rebuilt from the source URLs below.

No AI-generated visuals were used inside this post. The BerlinTools icon for the
post's widget is tracked separately in `tools-home/icons/_src/`.

Licence mix: 1 CC BY-SA 4.0 (cover), 1 CC BY-SA 4.0, 1 CC BY-SA 2.0, 1 CC BY 2.0,
2 CC BY-SA 3.0. Every selected file requires attribution, so a reader-facing
Image Credits block is required and present.

All six licences and author strings were re-verified directly against the
Commons API (`prop=imageinfo&iiprop=extmetadata`) on 2026-07-20, not taken from
the candidate-gathering notes.

Note on scarcity: photography and filming are forbidden on every Berliner
Unterwelten tour, so genuine interior images are rare on Commons. That is why
the one usable shelter interior below is treated as a high-value asset even
though its lighting is flat.

## Selected (6)

### 1. cover-humboldthain-flak-tower.jpg — FEATURED / COVER
- Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Flakturm_Volkspark_Humboldthain_Juli_2021_1.jpg
- Licence: CC BY-SA 4.0 · Author: Kasa Fue
- Original 5464x3640 → 1600x1066
- Shows: the Humboldthain flak tower ruin rising out of dense summer treetops,
  both viewing platforms with visitors on them, the northern Berlin skyline and
  the TV Tower on the horizon, July 2021.
- Why cover: it is the one selected image that reads instantly at listing-card
  size and still carries the article's argument. The ruin is visibly too big to
  remove and the park has grown over it, which is exactly the "Berlin buried
  this instead of demolishing it" point. Sharp, sunny, uncluttered.

### 2. brunnenstrasse-105-street-number.jpg
- Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Berliner_Unterwelten_(39900975650).jpg
- Licence: CC BY 2.0 · Author: Billie Grace Ward
- Original 7952x5304 → 1600x1067
- Shows: the enamel street plate `Brunnenstraße 105 / BERLINER UNTERWELTEN E.V.`
  screwed to the brick wall at the meeting point.
- Used for: the "the doors are not all at Gesundbrunnen" section. This is the
  single most useful image in the post, because the article's core practical
  warning is that 105 and 141 are two different places on the same street.

### 3. unterwelten-info-and-ticket-shop.jpg

### 4. unterwelten-tour-tickets-sign.jpg
- Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Berliner_Unterwelten.jpg
- Licence: CC BY-SA 2.0 · Author: Chris Alban Hansen
- Original 4032x3024 → 1600x1200
- Shows: the yellow and black `Eintrittskarten Führungen / Tour tickets` arrow
  sign mounted above a rounded concrete bunker top, blue winter sky behind.
- Used for: the ticket-window section. On-brand yellow, and it makes the point
  that the signage gets you to the door but not into the tour.

### 5. unterwelten-bunker-blast-doors.jpg
- Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Berliner_Unterwelten_(2).jpg
- Licence: CC BY-SA 3.0 · Author: SP2011
- Original 3072x2012 → 1600x1048
- Shows: a preserved shelter interior with two steel blast doors, exposed
  pipework, handrail and concrete steps.
- Used for: what is actually down there. Lighting is flat and even, which would
  normally be a mark against it, but it is a genuine interior of exactly the
  kind of space the article describes and such images are almost impossible to
  obtain because cameras are banned on the tours.

### 6. fichtebunker-kreuzberg-gasometer.jpg
- Commons: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Berlin,_Kreuzberg,_Fichtestrasse_4,_12,_Fichtebunker.jpg
- Licence: CC BY-SA 3.0 · Author: Jörg Zägel
- Original 3437x2341 → 1600x1090
- Shows: the round brick Fichtestraße gasometer of 1883/84 with the Berliner
  Unterwelten board at its entrance, sunny, blue sky.
- Used for: the Kreuzberg outlier. The shape is unlike anything else in the set,
  which is the point: this one is not part of a Wedding double-header.
- Parked cars along the front are a mild distraction, accepted because it is the
  clearest full view of the drum available and it is an honest street view.

## Rejected, with reasons

- `04`, `06` (Kasa Fue, CC BY-SA 4.0) — further aerials of the same flak tower.
  `04` is near top-down and reads as an abstract footprint rather than a place a
  reader can stand in. `06` has the better sky but the tower is too small in
  frame to work at card size. Both lose to `01`.
- `14` (Lukas Beck, CC BY-SA 4.0) — golden-hour portrait of the tower wall and
  the stair route. Beautiful light, but the subject is hard to read and the
  framing is awkward. Rejected on legibility, not on quality.
- `23` `Mythos Germania` permanent exhibition (F. Wüstenberg, CC BY-SA 4.0) —
  strong museum image, but it shows a separate permanent exhibition rather than
  any of the six guided tours this article is about. Rejected as misleading in
  context.
- `42` and other black-and-white archive frames showing civilians and children
  on shelter stairs — rejected. This is sensitive wartime imagery of identifiable
  people and the post is a practical booking guide, not a memorial piece.
- `30`, `31` curved bunker corridors — real and atmospheric but visually
  ambiguous; without a caption doing all the work they could be any basement.
- `33`, `34` `Pankstrasse` tiled station name walls — nice Berlin typography but
  they show the U-Bahn station, not the shelter behind it, and would imply the
  tour is on the platform.
- `27`, `28` Pankstraße station entrance and platform — merely available. They
  add nothing the text does not already say.
- `07`, `08`, `09`, `11`, `12`, `13`, `15`, `16`, `17` — assorted ground-level
  Humboldthain fragments: fences, stairs, graffiti, rubble, a single lump of
  concrete. Cluttered, dull, or unreadable as a subject.
- `10`, `18`, `35`, `36`, `38`, `40`, `41` — distant panoramas, empty streets,
  information boards and fenced ground. Weak or barely relevant.
- `22` shelter room with metal containers — genuine but odd as an illustration
  and easy to misread.
- `25`, `26`, `32`, `39` — further signage and building fronts already covered
  better by `03` and `04` in the selected set.
