## Selected (6 real images)

1. `01-giraffatitan-hall.jpg` — COVER + top of body.
   Giraffatitan brancai + Diplodocus in the museum's glass-roofed central hall,
   people at the base for scale. Warm, iconic, instantly readable at card size.
   Source: "Giraffatitan brancai, Berlin Natural History Museum (3)
   (39285312065)" — Richard Mortel from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia.
   Licence: CC BY 2.0. Wikimedia Commons.

2. `02-giraffatitan-portrait.jpg` — WIDGET anchor image (not a body node).
   Full towering Giraffatitan reaching the glass roof, crowd of visitors at the
   feet. Perfect real-photo scale anchor for the height-comparison widget.
   Source: "Berlin - Museum für Naturkunde - Brachiosaurus brancai" — Raimond
   Spekking. Licence: CC BY-SA 4.0. Wikimedia Commons. Credited inside the
   widget footer note.

3. `03-tristan-trex.jpg` — Tristan Otto, the real Tyrannosaurus rex skeleton.
   Source: "Tyrannosaurus rex at Museum für Naturkunde, Berlin" — Chris Alban
   Hansen. Licence: CC BY-SA 2.0. Wikimedia Commons.

4. `04-archaeopteryx-berlin-specimen.jpg` — The Berlin Archaeopteryx fossil slab.
   Source: "Archaeopteryx lithographica (Berlin specimen)" — H. Raab
   (User:Vesta). Licence: CC BY-SA 3.0. Wikimedia Commons.

5. `05-wet-collection.jpg` — The glowing amber wet collection (Nasssammlung),
   wall of alcohol-preserved specimens. One of the museum's most photographed
   rooms.
   Source: "Nasssammlung-Naturkundemuseum-Berlin-2023" — Tuxyso.
   Licence: CC BY-SA 4.0. Wikimedia Commons.

6. `06-museum-facade.jpg` — The Invalidenstraße 43 building exterior, used in the
   "how to visit" section.
   Source: "Museum für Naturkunde (36556352434)" — Shadowgate from Novara,
   Italy. Licence: CC BY 2.0. Wikimedia Commons.
